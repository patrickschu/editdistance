<Cromwell to Lord Stourton (1540),
<Life and Letters of Thomas Cromwell, ed. Merriman, no. 336, p. 249.

$I   Aftre my right hartie commendacion to your Lordshyp
$I Whereas I am informed that one John Compton of Yewill
$I haith in comparcenere with you a mylne and there of takyth
$I the moitie of the profetes and commodities to the same
$I belongyng and for as moche as the said john is dwelling and
$I abidyng nyghe vnto the same myll and your Lordshyp fer
$I from the vse and commoditie there of, contentid to receue the
$I rent of the one halfe, I shall desyer you at the contemplacion
$I of these my letters to grant vnto the said John Compton
$I thoccupacion of your moietie of the said myll by Leasse for
$I terme of XL yeres, or so many as ye can fynd in your hart to
$I bestow of hym conueniently at this my request paying there_fore
$I yerly so moche as heretofore it hathe been accustomed
$I by other your lordshypis tenantes And thus ye shall so do
$I vnto me thankfull plesure & in any your lordshypis lawfull
$I persutes ye shall requyer the same accordyngly. and thus
$I Fayr you right hartely well. From westmynstre thys xxiii
$I of February.
$I                        Your Lordshippis assuryd
$I                           Thomas Crumwell.
$IAdd. To my very good lord my Lord Sturton this be$I delivered
