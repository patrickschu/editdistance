<Queen Margaret to Cardinal Wolsey (1516),
<Original Letters, Illustrative of English History, ed. Ellis,
<vol. II, letter XLVIII, p. 130.

$I My Lorde Cardinal I comand me to you, and I
$I vould feyne have spokyn vyth you but ye var gon or I
$I coud cam to you and therfor I most vryt to you my
$I mynde. My Lorde I besche you to schw your gud
$I mynd to me, as ye have doon ever, but specyaly now,
$I for now ys the tyme. Me Lorde I pray you hartly to
$I get me som monne aganst nweyr day for ye knaw vell
$I I moust gyf part of rewards and other nedful thyngs
$I both for the kyng my brothers honour and myne: and
$I I schal not put you to no more troubul but I be sysch
$I you hartly my Lorde that I may have it to morow at
$I nyght at the farest: for ells I vyl be dysapointtyd. But
$I I put my hooll trust in you, and thys berar schal wayt
$I apon you for your ansuer as our Lord knouth wham
$I kype you
$I MARGARET R.
$I On to my Lord Kardenall.
