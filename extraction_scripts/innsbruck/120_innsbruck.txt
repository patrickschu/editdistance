<Edith Neville to Lady Plumpton (ca. 1505),
<Stapleton ed., Plumpton Correspondence, letter CLX, pp. 196-197.

$I To my Lady Plompton.
$I Myn own good Lady Plumpton, I recomende me unto you and
$I to your gud husband, and right sory I am of his and your
$I troubles. If I could remedy it, but God is where he was, and his
$I grace can and will poorvey every thing for the best, and help his
$I servant at their most needes, and so I trust his hynes, he wil do
$I you. My lord, my husband, recommends him unto you both, and
$I sends you yowr obblegasiyn; and has receyved but 4=li= and a
$I marke of the 20=li= and 2=li=: the remnant my lord gives your good
$I husband and you. And I pray Almighty Jesu send you both wel
$I to do, as your own herts can desire. Written in hast with the

<p. 197

$I hand of your mother, the 28 day of April.    Give credence to this
$I good bearer, for surely he love you full well.
$I Edith Nevill.
