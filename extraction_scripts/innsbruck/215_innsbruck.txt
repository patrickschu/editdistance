<(1583)

$I 16 October 1583.
$I Mr. Broughton, I have sent my servants Bagot and Trew
$I for to give you some warninge of my coming. I will lye wth you at
$I Warwik Inne. I hope to come to London both out of apparell and
$I money; therefore I pray you provide some against my coming.
$I I dare not bespeake my apparell because I feare King will mistake
$I my measure. I will have it made when I come. Thus readie to
$I take post horses, purposing to morowe to crosse Humber, I bid you
$I farewell, this xvjth of Octob. 1583.
$I Yors
$I (Signed) R. ESSEX.
$I I wilbe wth you the xxiijth of this moneth.
