<Henry Savill to William Plumpton (1545),
<Plumpton Correspondence, ed. Stapleton, no. 24, p. 250.

$I To his right worshipful Coussin, William Plompton, esquire, this
$I                          deliver.
$I   Cossin Plompton, after my hartie recommendations; your servant
$I sheweth me ye were and have bene very sick, wherof I am very
$I sory. And if your sicknes continue, wheare the commaundment
$I comes forth, send forth your servants and tennants, and send
$I forth your excuse to my Lord Lieutennant, with a letter of the
$I trough of your sicknes, and of the time of the continuance therof;
$I and being advertised of the truth, he will excep of your lawfull
$I and reasonable excuse. And thus hertely far you well. From
$I York, the xxviij of May, Anno 1545, 37 H. 8.
$I                             Your asurred kinsman,
$I                                               Henry Savill.

<[27 May 1545.]
