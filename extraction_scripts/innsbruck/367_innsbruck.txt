<Queen Henrietta Marie to Sir E. Nicholas (1641a),
<Memoirs of John Evelyn ... the private correspondence
<between King Charles I. and Sir Edward Nicholas, ed. Bray,
<Vol. V, p. 4.

$I Maistre Nicholas, I haue reseaued your letter: and
$I that you send me from the King: which writes me word
$I he as been vere well reseaued in scotland: and that
$I both the armie: and the people: have shued a creat joye
$I to see the King: and such: that theay say was neuer
$I seen before: pray god it may continued: for the letter
$I that I writt to you counserning the commissionaires it is
$I them that are toe dispatch bussinesse in the Kings ab_sence:
$I I thank you for your care of geuing me aduises
$I of what passes at London: and soe I reste
$I Your frand,
$I Henriette Marie R.
$I Otelands, the 19 August.
$I Indorsed, “For Mistre Nicholas."
$I In Sir E. N.'s writing:
$I “19=o= Aug. 1641.    The Queenes le=r= to me."
