<Litera Directa Domino de Hayles (1441),
<John Prior of Durham to Lord de Hales,
<The Priory of Coldingham, ed. Raine, p. 124.

$I   Right honorabill and worshipfull lord, I commend me to
$I you, thankand you of your graciouse supportacion doon to oure
$I celle of Coldingham, and to my brother John Oll, newly pre_sennt
$I to the said celle, besekand yowe of your goode conti_nuance.
$I And lyk itt you to remembre how att your request I
$I grannt the office of the bailyery of Coldingham to sir Alexan_der
$I Home for certeyn yhere3, if so be Sir Davy wald surren_dour
$I his lettres of the said office to me or to the prior of Cold_ingham,
$I and now late the said sir Alexander present your
$I honorabill lettres to me as touchand the same matere. And as
$I I wratt to your lordshipp befor, so wrytt I yhitt, thatt I and
$I my brether att your especiall request will grannt to the said Sir
$I Alexander the office of the said bailyery for lx yheere, so thatt
$I sir Davy will surrendour his lettres, as I wrate to you afore.
$I And Gode and our lady haff yow evermoore in thayre gra_ciouse
$I kepyng. Wreten at Durham, the xxiiii day of Novem_bre.
