<(1618),
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,

$I SWEET SISTER,
$I I must needs give you great thaynks for your
$I rare and bountefull present; no meat in the woorld
$I coold have ben so welcome. Evne at such a tyme
$I as I coold not bee pleased with any meat to pleas
$I my stomake, notwithstandyng I was sike of a che_ken
$I yester night, yet I coold eat thys with desyre,
$I and yet not sike of it, but my fitts never of mee.
$I God give me paciens. I pray God send you a
$I happy jorny and a safe and quik retorn. I have
$I adventured to send you thys poor playn cakes,
$I not so good as I wish thay were. Had I had any
$I thynge of worth, I woold in deed have .........
$I which in thees ragget lyns I seek to excuse. I
$I have so many charges as I am determined to let
$I my gowne aloane; but if you will doo mee the
$I favour to by mee so much meane stufe, canvas,
$I or what stufe you pleas, of black and whyt, to make
$I mee a wastcot for everye daye, you shall doo mee
$I a great favour. I have stufe by mee will serv for
$I thys loan plas, to make mee a playn gowne, If
$I you pleas to get mee a black fann bought, I will
$I leve troblyng of your La. but never leve lovyng
$I you. I wished, as I comanded thys bearer to tell
$I you, that you shoold not loose so much of your

<p. 55

$I presias tyme of entertaynyng and injoying so
$I honorable a parsnach, but put it in your poket till
$I you come at London; and so I bid you a harty
$I farwell, resting your La'ps lovyng unfortunat syster,
$I MARY BATHON.
$I xx of May [1618].
$I I am bold to send thees letters to which of your
$I attendans you pleas to comand, to get them dely_vered.

$I To my lovyng lionorable syster, the La. Cornwaleys, give these.
