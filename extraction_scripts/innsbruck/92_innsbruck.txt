<The Cely Letters, ed. Hanham, pp. 75-6.

$I   Ryught interly whellebelouyd and my syngeler good brother,

<p. 76

$I thynke, enformyng [you] at the makyng of thys howr father and
$I mother, my godfather Maryon, and whe awll wher in good heyll,
$I thankyd be the good Loord. Syr, hyt is so be grehyt labor that
$I the whoman that howr brother Robard whos tangyllyd wyth, sche
$I has made hyme a qwyetans, and sche has aull her awne good that
$I was browhyt to howr brothers ageyn, and aull the good that howr
$I brother levyd wyth her, saue a gyrdyll of goulde wyth the bokyll
$I and pendawnte sylluer and gylte, and a lyttyl golde ryng wyth
$I a lyttyll dyamond, and a typete of damaske. Sche has awll hother
$I thyngys that he leuyd wyth her, and wyll haue. Syr, howr father
$I and mother wolde that 3e payd for hys bord at Calles and delyuer
$I hym v s. [or mor] in hys pors, and 3e to take a byll of hys hande of
$I as mwche mony as 3e lay houte for hyme; and whe wolde that
$I he wolde come to Hawelay and be ther tyll the mater be better
$I hessyd. Howr father thynkys he neddys not to be large of spendyng,
$I remembyryng aulleth[y]ng. Syr, I pray you lette hyme not se thys
$I letter ne tell hym note of tys 3end byt of the qwetans, and hy hyme
$I to Hawelay in as gret haste as ye can. No mor to you at tys tyme.
$I Jhesu kepe you. Wryttyn at London the xv day of Maye. Syr,
$I I pray yow send my doblet by hym, or be the nexte frend that com.
$I Hyt ys not for hyme to come in London 3eyte.
$I                              per yowr brother,
$I                                 Rychard Cely.

$I Dorse: Wnto my ryught whelbelouyd brother George Cely,
$I merchand of the Estapell at Callys, be thys dd.
