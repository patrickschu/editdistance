$I MOST DEARE SlSTER,
$I I HAVE receved that money which you was
$I pleased to send me by Mr Greenhill, which came
$I soe conveniently as I could wish. The shipping
$I is now come which I have soe long expected; and
$I on Saturday next I meane, with God's permission,
$I to goe for the Low Countries. I have been much
$I beholding unto my Lady Cornwalies, who was

<p. 266

$I pleased to come and see me, and after brought me
$I to kiss the King and Queen's hands. The King
$I is gone for Scotland; and for the other nuse att
$I court concerning Mrs Villiers and Mr Garman, I
$I thinke you will heare the relacion of it before these
$I shall come unto you, so that I shall not neede fur_ther
$I to write to you of it. Deare Sister, I give
$I you thankes for all your real favours, and all hap_pines
$I attend you and all yours; and soe, being
$I called away by the hasty marriners, I must desist
$I to write, but never to love you.
$I Yor most assured in all true affection,
$I          ANNA MEAUTYS.
$I From London, May 9, 1633.
$I To my deare and much honoured sister, Lady Bacon.
$I Favor me, sweete Sister, soe much to recom_mend
$I my kind respectes unto my sweete neveu and
$I my neece; and, if you will please to kisse pretie
$I Charles and Hercules for my sake, I shall be your
$I servant.
