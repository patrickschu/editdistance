<Cromwell to Lord Hungerford (1540),
<Life and Letters of Thomas Cromwell, ed. Merriman, no. 335, p. 248.

<Feb. 9 [1540].

$I   Thies be for asmoche as the Kinges Majestie hathe ben
$I enformed that certaine lycences foundacions and other wry_tynges
$I belongyng unto the late priory of Charterhous Henton
$I and graunted by Thomas Horton Clothier and other hathe
$I ben ymbeysted and conveyd away by certaine persons. The
$I Kinges Majesties pleasure is that ye calling Dame Mary
$I Horton Wydow, Thomas Horton, William Byrde clerke
$I Richard Davis and Sir William Furber clerke before you shall
$I examine therin and every of theym by vertue of an othe to be
$I admynestred to theym by you of and uppon the premysses
$I with the circumstaunces therof by all the wayes and meanes
$I ye can or may excogitate and device and to advertise me of
$I that ye shall fynd and knowe therin with all convenyent spede
$I to thentent I may cause suche order and direction to be had
$I and taken in that behalfe as the case shall requyre. Faile ye
$I not thus to do as the Kinges trust and expectations ys
$I in you.
$I   London, 9 Feb.

<Signed. Add.
