<John Wilmot, Earl of Rochester, to his wife (1672),
<The Letters of John Wilmot, ed. Treglown, p. 82.

$I      From our Tubb att M=ns= Fourcards this 18nt of Octob:
$I    Wife; Our gutt has allready binn griped, & wee are now in bed
$I soe that wee are not in a condition of writing either according to
$I thy merritt or our desert, wee therefore doe command thy
$I benigne acceptance of these our letters in what way soever by us
$I inscribed or directed, willing thee therewithall to assure our sole
$I daughter & heire issue female, the Lady ann part. of our best
$I respects, this with your care & dilligence, in the erection of our
$I furnaces is att present the utmost of our will & pleasure
