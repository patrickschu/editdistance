<(1618),
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,

$I DEAR CORNWALLIS,
$I You may see how unable they are to dispose of
$I their own tymes that attend debts and other
$I occasions, by my no sooner sending you word when
$I I will be with you, which now I intend, by God's
$I permission, to be on Tuesday com se'night att
$I night, & staying with you all Wensday; but on
$I Thursday you must give me leave to retorne
$I homewards, for I must needs be hear againe on
$I Friday night. I wright this in extream haste,
$I therefore excuse me that I saye no more but that
$I I will ever be found
$I Yr most affectionat & faithfull freind,
$I L. BEDFORD.
$I Bedford House, this 7th of May [1618].
$I To my honorable frend the Lady Cornewallis.
