$I Bedford House, this of Desember [1615].

$I DEARE CORNEWALLIS,

$I BECAUSE your woeman went so sodainely out of
$I the towne as my letters fayled comming tyme
$I enough to goe by her, I send this bearer, by whos
$I jorney I shall not only have the means to bring
$I myselfe to your remembrance, whear I desier to
$I live as your most affectionat freind, but the con_tentment
$I to hear how you and yours doe. If I
$I might also by him understand that Mr Bacon and
$I you wold shortly be in towne, itt wold be very
$I wellcome newse to me that am like to be a Lon_doner
$I the most of this winter, to ayer my house at
$I Twicknam against the spring. I am shuer the

<p. 34

$I busnesses now a foote hear flie over all the king_dom,
$I and therfore cannot be unknown to you;
$I yett Sir Thomas Monson's being sent this morn_ing
$I to the Tower perhaps will not be so soone
$I with you by any other hand as by this letter;
$I therfore itt tells you of that, and that the change
$I of his prison is a signe ther is more to be laid to
$I his Charge then what consernes Overbury's death
$I only. My La. of Somersett is not yett brought
$I to bed, but this is her last day of reckoning.
$I Whear and when this tragedy will ende I thinke
$I God only knows; to whom, with my best devotion,
$I I co(ende you, and beg of you that no omission
$I of seremonis may make you thinke me the lesse
$I Your faithfull freind,
$I L. BEDFORD.

$I Sweet Madam, comend me to Mr Bacon and
$I continue me in his good opinion, which I will be
$I ever ready to deserve by any office of an affec_tionat freind.
