<The Queen of Bohemia to Sir E. Nicholas (1654),
<Memoirs of John Evelyn, ed. Bray, vol. V, pp. 196-198.

$I Hagh, Sep: 7 (1654).
$I Mr. Secretarie, I thanke you both for the good news
$I you writt to S=r= Charles Cottrell out of Holland, and for
$I your letter I receaued this morning with the relation of the
$I defeat before Arras. I hope you will send that of Holland
$I to Curtius that the beleef of the Scotch defeat may not be

<p. 197

$I continued in Germanie, but none pleaseth me better then
$I what you write of my deere Godsonne, and the continuance
$I of my Neeces good health. The Queen of Sweden is yett
$I at Anwerp, wee looke euerie day to see the Landgrave
$I heere, and by him I shall know what she will doe. It is
$I certaine that the flux is much in Monkes army, a Scotch_man
$I that is come from thence reports it, and all the parti_culars
$I you haue written. Dr. Morley has a letter from
$I Anwerp of some trouble by a discouerie of a new treason in
$I London of the levellers against his pretious highness, but I
$I beleeue you will haue more particullars of that then wee
$I heere. Dr.Earle setts forwards to morrow to Breda and
$I so to Aix. I doe not write to you by him because this will
$I be sooner with you. Our Baron has sent for his man Smith
$I o meet him God knows where, for I doe not, I beleeve
$I you will haue him at Aix: he is the direct wandring Jew.
$I My Ladie Herbert is looked for heere shortlie, but she was
$I not come from Paris the last week. I heare M=rs= Hide is

<p. 198

$I to come to my Neece in Mrs Killegrews place, which I am
$I verie glad of, she is verie fitt for itt and a great fauorit of
$I mine, who ame euer
$I Your most affectionat
$I Frend,
$I I pray lett my Lord Wentworth know I ame extreme
$I glade he is of the kings councell, being so much his frend
$I as I ame I cannot but wish him much ioye of it.
$I Indorsed, 7=o= Sep=bris= 1654.    The Queene of Bohemia to me.
