<Mary Queen of Scots to Sir Francis Knollys (1568),
<Original Letters, ed. Ellis, Vol. II,
<LETTER CXCVI, pp. 253-254.

$I Mester Knoleis, y heuu har sum neus from
$I Scotland; y send zou the double off them y vreit to
$I the quin my gud sister, and pres zou to du thelyk,
$I conforme to that y spak zesternicht vnto zou, and sut
$I hesti ansur y refer all to zour discretion, and wil lipne
$I beter in zour gud delin for mi, nor y kan persuad
$I zou, nemli in this langasg excus my iuel vreitin for
$I y neuuer vsed it afor, and am hestet. Ze schal si my
$I bel vhuilk is opne, it is sed Seterday my unfrinds
$I wil bi vth zou, y sey nething bot trests weil, and ze
$I send oni to zour wiff ze mey asur her schu wald a
$I bin weilcom to apur strenger, hua nocht bien
$I aquentet vth her, wil nocht bi ouuer bald to vreit

<p. 254

$I bot for the aquentans betuix ous. Y wil send zou
$I letle tekne to rember zou off the gud hop y heuu
$I in zou, guef ze fend a mit mesager y wald wysh
$I ze bestouded it reder apon her non ani vder; thus
$I effter my commendations y prey God heuu zou in
$I his kipin.
$I Zour asured gud frind
$I MARIE R.
$I  Excus my iuel vreitin 
$I thes furst tym.
