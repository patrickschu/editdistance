<The Queen of Bohemia to Sir E. Nicholas (1654),
<Memoirs of John Evelyn, ed. Bray, vol. V, pp. 203-204.

$I The Queen of Bohemia to Mr. Secretary Nicholas.
$I Hagh Oct. 2. (1654.)
$I Mr. Secretarie, I send you heere a letter for the King,
$I it is about a match betwixt Prince Adolphe the King of
$I Suedes brother and Sophie; he has desired it verie hand_somlie:
$I my sonne has consented to it, reseruing the King
$I of Suedens consent and mine, who ame to acquaint the
$I King with it. I doe it now, and send you the copie of
$I Prince Adolphes letter, I pray gett an answere from the

<p. 204

$I King as soone as you can. I haue no more to say, but
$I ame euer
$I Your most affectionat frend.
$I I pray assure me to my Lo: Wentworth, I write not
$I now to him, I haue no time, for the post is readie to goe.
$I I pray say the same to reuerent Dick Harding.
$I “2=o= 8=bris= 1654. st. No. R. 4=°=. The Queene of Bohemia to
$I me concerning y=e= Kings consent for Presse Sophia to marry
$I Pr=ce= Adolph, y=e= King of Swedens brother."
