<Margaret Queen of Scotland (1516),
<Original Letters, Illustrative of English History, ed. Ellis,
<vol. II, letter XLVII, p. 129.

$I Derest broder as hartly as I can I recomend me
$I onto you, and let you vyt that yesternyght I cam
$I hyther, soo beyng confortted of you in my jornay in
$I many and soondry wyses that, lovyng be to our Lorde
$I God, I am in ryght good heal, and as joyous of my
$I  sayd Jornay towarde you as ony woman may be in
$I  commyng to her broder, as I have gret cause, and
$I am moost desirous now to com to your presens and
$I to have sight of your person, in whom next God, is
$I myn oonly trust and confydens: advertissing you
$I derest brotder I have reseved thys day a Letter from
$I my soons ambassadors, now at London, which Leter
$I I send on to you heryinne closed, and have adressed
$I on to them myn ansuer severally in twoe sondery
$I lettres, copy wher of I all so sende on to you, that
$I apon notice had of the same it may lyke you to co_mand
$I whether of the said letters ye seme besst at
$I your plessur shall be delyverd. And the Holy Tre_nyte
$I have you my most derest broder in tuycion and
$I governance. At Stony Stretford the xxvij. day of
$I Apryll.
$I Zour loveng suster
$I MARGARET.
$I Unto my dereste broder the
$I King's Grace.
