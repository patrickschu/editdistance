<Robert, 2nd Earl of Essex, to Hugh Broughton
<Devereux Papers with Richard Broughton's Memoranda (1575-1601)
<ed. Malden
<number 19, pp. 22-23.
<(1581)

$I 15 April 1581.
$I Mr. Broughton, I receaved yor l'res by my man Antony by
$I the wch I perceave you have gotten the LL. consents for my cominge
$I up to London and that you will send yor man and horses against
$I St. Georgs daye. I pray you, against I come up, let me have one
$I verie faire suite of apparell against St. George day ; satten doublet,
$I velvet hose, and jirkin of crimson laid on wth silver lace, wth my
$I foote-cloth, my mens lyveries, etc. Thus wth my hartie comen_dacons
$I to yor self, yor partner, I end. In hast, goinge to beide,
$I this xvth of April 1581.
$I Yors assured
$I (Signed) R. ESSEX.

<p. 23

$I I will send some stuff this weke unto London wch you may loke
$I for on Friday by Hobson the carier of Cambridge.
