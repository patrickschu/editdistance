<Queen Henrietta Marie to Sir Edward Nicholas (1641b),
<Memoirs of John Evelyn ... the private correspondence
<between King Charles I. and Sir Edward Nicholas, ed. Bray,
<Vol. V, pp. 99-100.

$I Maistre Nicholas, I have receaued your lettre. I am
$I sory you ar not well for I would haue ben glad to speake
$I to you but it is of no haste therefore donat hastend your
$I selfe for feare of being sick; I send you a lettre for Mi_lord
$I Keeper that the King ded send to me to deliuer it if
$I I though it fit. the subject of it is to make a Derclara_tion
$I against the ordres of Parliamant which ar made
$I withouct the King. If you beleue a fit time giue it
$I him if not you may keepet till I see you.
$I the King will bee here sertaineleye the 20 of this
$I monthe therefore you may aduertice the Maior of Lon_don.
$I Your lettre that you did writt to Carnaruen is
$I comme bak to mee and I burnt it. he was not at is

<p. 100

$I hows it should be vere nessessairie that you should in_quaire
$I where (he) is and writt to him and send to milord
$I Cotinton for is proxies for I heer he as to (two) and is
$I owne. and send to milord Southampton and Dunse_moure
$I to send their proxies till the comme them selues;
$I the are in Warwicshier. hauing no more to say I reste
$I this 12 Novembre
$I Your assured frend
$I Henriette Marie R.
$I "For your selfe.”
$I 12=o= N=obris= 1641.
$I The Queene to me.
