<Litera Directa Comiti D’Angus (1441),
<John Prior of Durham to a fellow of Angus,
<The Priory of Coldingham, ed. Raine, pp. 123-24.

$I   Right honorabill and worshipfull lorde, I comend me to your
$I lordship, thankand yow of the goode grace tendirnesse and
$I supportacion whilke yhe haff doon and dayly doose to oure
$I celle of Coldingham, besekyng you evermoor of your graciouse
$I continuance. And as touchand the office of the bailyery of
$I Coldyngham, to the whilke yhe desyre to be promote Sir Alex_ander
$I Home, your welbelovyd cosyn, lik itt your said lordshipp
$I to undirstande that before michaelmesse last past Sir Davy
$I Home was att Durham, to wham I and my brethere grannte

<p. 124

$I the office of the balyery of Coldingham for xl yheere, under
$I certeyn condicion3, whe thatt tyme noght knawand na conande
$I ne pacte made betwixt Sir Alexander and Sir Davy. Never_thelesse
$I if the said Sir Davy Home will discharge hym selfe
$I and surrendour his lettres to me, or to the Priour of Coldyng_ham
$I I can well agree me for your sake and att your requeste
$I to grannte the said office of the said bailyery to sir Alexander
$I Home for xl yheere, upon certeyn condicon3. And Gode and
$I his blessid modre and oure haly patron seynt Cuthbert ever_moore
$I haff you in thayre haly keepynge. Writen at Durham,
$I the xxiiii day of Novembr.
