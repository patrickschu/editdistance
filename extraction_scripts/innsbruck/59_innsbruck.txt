<Alice, Duchess of Suffolk, to William Stonor (5 March,? 1475)
<The Stonor Letters and Papers 1290 – 1483, ed. Charles Lethbridge Kingsford, Vol. I
<Letter 148, p. 154.

$I Right trusty and entierly beloved ffrende we grete you well, desiryng
$I and praying yow, all excuses layde apart, that incontinent this lettur
$I seyne ye come to us to Ewelme for certayne grete causes concernyng
$I our wele and pleasir, whiche at your comyng ye shall undrestond more
$I pleynely: and theruppon ye to departe ayen at your pleasir, so that ye
$I fayle not here ynne at this tyme as our parfait trust ys in you: and as
$I in gretter case we woll be gladde to do for you, that knoweth our lorde,
$I who have you ever yn gouvernaunce. Wreten at Ewelme the v day of
$I Marche • 3 •
$I Alyce Suffolk.
$I To our Right trusty and entierly beloved ffrende Wyllyam Stoner.
