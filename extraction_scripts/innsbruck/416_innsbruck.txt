<Elizabeth, Countess of Chesterfield, to Lady Giffard (1664),
<Martha Lady Giffard: her Life and Correspondence, ed. Longe, p. 15.

$I MY DEARE FRIEND, - I expected to have heard from
$I you many possts since in answer to a letter of mine
$I wherein I desyred to know when I should send my
$I choch to Northampton for you. I heare it miscarryed
$I and so am writing againe to trouble you with the same
$I question. I hope by this time y=r= sister is brought to
$I bed and very well and that you will noe longer delaye
$I me of a happynesse I cannot be satisfied without. I
$I am now all alone and am like to be soe to my Lord's
$I bussnyes keeping him in Towne. I knew nothing of
$I returning in to Ireland and I doe believe I never shall,
$I being very well settled heare and perfectly contented  I
$I shall be when you will bee soe good as to performe the
$I promisse you have maide to my deare friend.-YOURS.
$I Aug. the 10th, 1664.
