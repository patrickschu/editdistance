<Agnes Plumpton to Sir Robert Plumpton (1503),
<Plumpton Correspondence, ed. Stapleton, pp. 184-85.

$I To the worshipfull Robart Plompton, knight, be this byll delivered
$I                             in hast.
$I   Right worshipfull Sir, in my most hartie wyse I recomend me
$I unto you, evermore desiring to here of your prosperytie and well-faire,
$I and good sped in your matters, shewyng you that I and all
$I your children is in good health (blessed be Jesu) and prays you for
$I your blessing. Sir it is so now that I have made you thewsans
$I of the money, that ye sent to me for, and I have sent it you with
$I John Walker at this tyme; the which I shall shew you how I mayd
$I shift of, at your comminge. And I pray you that ye be not miscon_tent
$I that I sent it no sooner, for I have made the hast that I could
$I that was possible for me to do. And also, Sir, I will not lett Tho:
$I Croft wife plow nor occupie her fermeald, but saith she shall not
$I occupy without yer life. And also I pray you to send me word
$I how you speed in your matters againe, as soon as ye may; and
$I also to send me word where ye will your horses to come to you.

<p. 185

$I No more at this time, but the Trinity keep you. From Plumpton
$I in hast, the xix=th= day of March.
$I                                           By your wife,
$I       (19 March 1503-4.)                     Dame AGNES PLOMPTON.
