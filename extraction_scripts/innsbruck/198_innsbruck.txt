<Letter of Warrant from Queen Elizabeth to Sir Thomas
<Smith and Dr. Wilson, for putting two of the Duke
<of Norfolk's Servants to the rack (1571)
<Original Letters, ed. Ellis, Vol. II,
<LETTER CC, pp. 261-262.

$I ELIZABETH R.         By the Quene.
$I Right trusty and welbeloved we grete yow well,
$I and fyndyng in the traytoroos attempts lately disco_vered
$I that nether Barker nor Bannister the Duke of
$I Norfolks men have uttred ther knolledg, nother will
$I discover the same without torture; forasmuch as
$I the knolledg herof concerneth our suerty and estate,
$I and that they have untruly allredy answered, We will
$I and by warrant herof authoriss you to procede to the
$I furder examination of them uppon all poynts that you
$I can thynk by your discretions mete for knolledg of the
$I truth. And, they shall not seme to yow to confess
$I playnly ther knolledg, than we warrant yow to cause
$I them both, or ether of them, to be brought to the rack:

<p. 262

$I and first to move them with feare therof to deale
$I playnly in ther answers, and if that shall not move
$I them than yow shall cause them to be putt to the
$I rack, and to find the tast therof untill they shall deale
$I more playnly, or untill yow shall thynk mete. And
$I so we remitt the whole procedyng to your furder dis_cretion,
$I requiryng yow to use spede herin and to re_quire
$I the assistance of our Lieutenant of the Toure.
$I Gyven under our signet the xv=th=. of Septemb. 1571.
$I To o=r= trustie and right well beloved
$I Councellors S=r=. Thomas..... yth K=t=. and
$I to o=r= .....tie and welbeloved Doc_tor
$I ....... son one of the Masters of our
$I Requestes.

<Indorsed
$I Receaved at the Towir the xvj. daie of 7=ber=. at eleven of the clocke in the forenoone 1571.
