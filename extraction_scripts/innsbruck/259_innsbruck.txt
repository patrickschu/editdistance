<Robert Daborne to Mr. Hinchlow (1613),
<The Alleyn Papers, ed. Collier, pp. 56-57.

$I    Memorandum: 'tis agreed between Phillip Hinchlow, Eq=r=
$I and Robert Daborn, gent., y=t= y=e= s=d= Robert shall before y=e= end
$I of this Easter Term deliver in his Tragoedy, cald Matchavill
$I and y=e= Divill, into the hands of y=e= sd Phillip, for y=e= summ of
$I xxty pounds, six pounds whearof y=e= sd Robert aknowledgeth
$I to hav receaved in earnest of y=e= sayd play this 17=th= of Aprill,

<p. 57

$I and must hav other fowr pound upon delivery in of 3 acts. and
$I other ten pound upon delivery in of y=e= last scean p~fited. In
$I witnes hearof the s=d= Robert Daborn hearunto hath set his hand
$I this 17=th= of Aprill, 1613.
$I                                      P=r= me, Rob. Daborne.
$I    Mem. I have receaved of M=r= Hinchlow the full somm of
$I sixteen pounds, in part of twenty pounds due to me, Robert
$I Daborne, for my tragoedy of Matchavill and the Divell: I say
$I receaved sixteen pounds, this 19=th= of may as aforesaid. In
$I witnes whearof I herunto hav set my hand, 1613.
$I                                        ROBT. DABORNE.
$I This play to be deliverd in to
$I    M=r= Hinchlow with all speed.
$I                       JOHN ALLEYN.
