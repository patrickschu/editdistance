<Cecily Marchioness of Dorset to Thomas Cromwell (1523),
<Original Letters, Illustrative of English History, ed. Ellis,
<vol. II, letter LXXVIII, p. 219.

$I Cromwell, I woll that yow send to me in hast the
$I trussynn bed of cloth of tyssewe, and the fether bed,
$I wyth the fustyons and a materas longynn to the same
$I wyth the cownterpoynt; also I woll that you delyver
$I all such tents, pavylyons, and hales as you have of
$I myne on to my sonne Lenard, as you tender my ple_sur.
$I And thys shall be your suffycyant warrant and
$I dyscharge att all tymes. Wrytyn at Bedwell thys
$I present Thorysdaye by foore our Lady daye the As_sumpcyon.
$I CECYL DORSETT.
