<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<(1630-31)

$I TO Or RIGHT TRUSTIE AND RIGHT WELL BELOVED
$I THE LADY BACON.
$I HENRIETTE MARIE R.
$I RIGHT trustie and right well beloved, we greete
$I you well. Wee are so sensible of the respect
$I that you have shewed to or request, in receyving

<p. 237

$I againe your sonne into yor favor, that wee cannot
$I chuse but let you know that wee take it very
$I thankfully at your hands. If wee can prevaile
$I but this much further with you, that you will
$I extend tbe same kindnes towards your daughter
$I in law, and so receive them both into yor motherly
$I care, you shall put such an obligation upon us as
$I wee shall never forget, but remember upon all
$I occasions wherein our favour can be of any use
$I unto you; and, with this assurance, wee committ
$I you to the protection of the Altmightie.
$I Given under or hand at Newmarket, this one
$I and thirty day of January [1630-31].
