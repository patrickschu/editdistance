<Anne Lady Bacon to Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<(1613),
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<number 17, p. 22.

$I Yor delaye in cominge downe hath caused
$I you once againe to be trobled with my deer Nathl
$I at London. I am sorry that your busynes doth
$I carye you another way that I shall not see you
$I at Culford in your jorney towards Broome. But it
$I shall not be longe I trust in God before I see
$I you in a neerer affinity then yett ever I did, in
$I which I joye muche now; and I doubt not but
$I you shall muche more joye in it, when I am with
$I the Lord God in Heaven. My husband commends
$I hym very kindly unto you, and we bothe doe
$I very willingly give unfo you the juill of our deer
$I sonne. God blesse you together with abbundance
$I of all felicity in this lyfe, and in the lyfe to come
$I the presence of the Blessed Trinity, a greater
$I happiness cannot be to any. Deer La. the bless_ed
$I God be with you for ever. Yors,
$I ANNE BACON.

$I [1613-14.]
$I To my most deerly beloved the Lady Jane Cornwallis give this, at
$I at London.
