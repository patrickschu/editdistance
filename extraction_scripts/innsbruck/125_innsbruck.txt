<Mary the French Queen to her brother King Henry VIII. (ca. 1514),
<Original letters illustrative of English history, ed. Henry Ellis, vol. 2,
<Letter XCIX, pp. 304-305.

$I My most derest and best belowyd brother I hum_bly
$I recommend vn to yowr grace. Sir so yt tys that I
$I have bene very seke and eke atons for the wyche I
$I was fayne to sende for Master Peter the fesysyon for
$I to haue hoplen me of the dessays that I haue. How
$I be yt I am rathar wors than better. Were for I
$I trowst showrly  to come vp to Londone with my Lord
$I . For and yf I shold tary here I am sowr I shold never
$I asspeare the sekenys that I haue. Wer for, Sir, I wolde
$I be the gladther a grete dele to com thether, by cawse
$I I wold be glad to se yowr grace, the wyche I do
$I thyncke long for to do; for I haue bene a grete wyle
$I out of yowr syte: and now I thowst I shal not be so
$I long agene: for the syte of yowr grace ys to me the
$I grettys comforte to me that may be possybel. No

<p. 305

$I more to your grace at thys tyme, bwt I pray God send
$I yow yowr hartys dessyr, and showrly to the syte of yow.
$I by yowr lowyng suster
$I MARY THE FRENCHE QU..
