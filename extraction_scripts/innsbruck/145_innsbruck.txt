$I                                Woking
$I                            1 September [1523]
$I   Hit may lyke your good Grace to be aduertised that
$I according to your Gracis commaundement, geven me by your
$I lettres dated the xxxth day of Auguste, I haue shewed vn to the

<p. 279

$I Kingis Grace the byll devised for Sir Richard Wyngfeld, sub_scribed
$I by your Grace, and the old bill, also aduertisyng his Grace
$I of such thingis as your Grace in the new bill caused to be lefte
$I owte for thadvantage of his Highnes, which pointis I shewed his
$I Grace cancelled in the old bill and omitted in the new, ffor which
$I his Highnes, with hartie thankis to your Grace for your labor
$I taken therin, hath signed the new, which I haue delivered to hym
$I of whom I received hit.
$I   And thus our Lord long preserve your good Grace in honour and
$I helth. At Okyng the ffyrst day of Septembre.
$I     Your humble orator and moost bounden bedeman
$I                                             [Thomas More]
