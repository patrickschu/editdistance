<Dame Elizabeth Stonor to Sir William Stonor (May 1478)
<The Stonor Letters and Papers 1290 – 1483, ed. Charles Lethbridge Kingsford, Vol. II
<Letter 208, p. 45

$I . . . þerto [a] sponefull . . . half a nottemegge in powder, and 
$I drynke hem togeddyrs and ye shall with Godes grace ffynde greate 
$I ease þerin. I marvell that ye wold not send ffor noþinge to helpe you after 
$I your tallynge : be my trouth I knew not þeroff till now by your writynge, 
$I and yff I had knowen it I wold not have bene mery, nor nomore I am 
$I not, nor shall not till I see you, the which by my good will shalbe 
$I shortely assone as I can make anend. My tariynge is now ffor to have 
$I my dedes here off London and Stepynhith seelid by syr Harry Fenne, 
$I Barnewell and Wallesse. Barnewell is not in towne : he will be here 
$I shortely, and I trust to speke off all þinges in that matter with godes 
$I grace. Syr Harry Fenne and Fyncham be resonabely well kee'yd : þei 
$I be not so hoote as they were: and yet they wilbe better hereafter I 
$I dowte not. And I praye you hartely gentill [l]offynge husbond to be 
$I mery and take in conseyte with you in your . . . ease . . . with [go]des 
$I grace it shall as lyghtly p[ass] as it come and trewly ....
$I To my most worshipfull and best beloved husband William Stonor.
