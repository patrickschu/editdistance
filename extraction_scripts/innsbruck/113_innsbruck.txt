<Agnes Plumpton to Sir Robert Plumpton (1504c),
<Plumpton Correspondence, ed. Stapleton,
<Letter 153, p. 188.

$I     To the worshipfull Robart Plompton, kt. be thes byll delivered
$I                             in hast.
$I   Right worshipfull, I in most hartee wyse recomennd me unto
$I you, desiring to heare of your wellfaire and good speed in your
$I matters, letting you understand that I am in good helth, with all
$I your children (blessed be Jesu), and pray you of your daly bless_inge;
$I and all your servants is in good health and is right glad to
$I here of your welfare. Sir, on the eving after the making of this
$I letter, your servant Edmund Robyson come home, and so I un_derstond
$I by your letter, that you wold understand if sir John Ro_clife
$I servants have received any ferme in Yorkshire, but therof I
$I can get no knowledg as yet. But they have sold oke wood at
$I Nesfeld, and lettes them stand to the tyme of the yere, one oke
$I that is worth xl=d= for xij=d=; and also they have sold aches at the
$I same place: and the okes are sold to William Clapame and Rich_ard
$I Clapame, and the aches to the towards there about. And
$I also at Idell, they have sold holyn to James Formes and to Tho_mas
$I Quentin and William Aches, and herof I can geet no more
$I certaintie as yet. And also there is no mo of your tenaunts to get
$I as yet, nor your servants nether, at this tyme; but the Trenitie
$I have you in his blessed keepinge      Scrybbled in hast, the fryday
$I next after St Marke day.
$I                                       By your wyffe,
$I       (26 April 1504.)                     Dame AGNES PLOMPTON.
