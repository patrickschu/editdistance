<Queen Anne Boleyn to Thomas Cromwell (1824),
<Original Letters, ed. Ellis, Vol. II,
<LETTER CXVI, p. 46.

$I Anne the Quene. By the Quene.
$I Trustie and right welbiloued we grete you
$I well. And where as we be crediblie enformed that the
$I berer hereof Richard Herman marchaunte and citizen
$I of Antwerpe in Brabant was in the tyme of the late
$I lorde Cardynall put and expelled frome his fredome
$I and felowshipe of and in the Englishe house there, for
$I nothing ells (as he affermethe) but oonly for that that
$I he dyd bothe with his gooddis and pollicie, to his
$I greate hurte and hynderans in this Worlde, helpe to
$I the settyng forthe of the News Testamente in Eng_lisshe.
$I We therefore desire and instantly praye you
$I that with all spede and favoure convenient ye woll
$I cause this good and honeste marchaunt, being my
$I Lordis true faithfull and loving subjecte, restored to
$I his pristine fredome, libertie, and felowshipe aforesaid,
$I and the soner at this oure requeste, and at your good
$I leyser to here hym in suche thinges as he hathe to
$I make further relacion unto you in this behalf. Yeven
$I undir our Signete at my Lordis manoure of Grene_wiche
$I the xiiij=th=. daye of May.
$I To our trustie and  right welbeloved
$I Thomas Crumwell squyer Chief Secretary
$I unto my Lorde the Kings Highnes.
