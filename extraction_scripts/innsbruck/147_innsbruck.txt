<The Princess Mary to Secretary Cromwell (1824),
<Original Letters, ed. Ellis, Vol. II,
<LETTER CXI, pp. 29-30.

$I MARYE PRINCESSE.
$I Maister Cromewell I commende me to you, and
$I hertely thanke you for your kyndenes and favor lately
$I shewed at my desyre unto my servante Richard Wil_bram
$I in the bihalfe of his father. I nowe eftsones de_sire
$I and hertely praye you to shewe your laufull favor
$I and goodnes unto Sir Rice Mauncell this beirer, in
$I suche matters and buysenes as he is a suter unto you;
$I for the said Sir Rice hathe maried oon of my gentil_women,
$I whome, for her long and acceptable service to
$I me done, I myche esteme and favor. Wherfore if ye
$I wolde, at this my desyre, extende your goodnes and

<p. 30

$I favor unto him in that bihalfe, so that he myght per_ceive
$I his said suit th'rather therby to take good ef_fecte,
$I ye theryn shulde mynestre unto me veraye ac_ceptable
$I pleasor, whiche I shall herafter remembre ac_cordingly.
$I From Otforde the xxvij=th=. daye of June.
