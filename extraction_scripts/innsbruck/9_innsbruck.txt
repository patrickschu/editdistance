<King Henry V to the Aldermen of London (1417b),
<A book of London English, eds. Chambers & Daunt pp. 67-68.

$I Littera domini Regis Maiori & Aldremannis missa.

$I   By the Kyng -- Right trusty and welbeloued, We grete you
$I often tymes wel, doyng you to vndirstonde that, on Seint Cuth_bertes
$I day the Translacion, god of his high grace send vn-to
$I oure handes oure town of Caen by assault, and with right litell
$I deth of oure peple, wherof we thanke our saviour lowelich
$I as we can or may, prayng you that ye do the same, and as de_uoutely
$I as ye can; certefying you also that we and our host been
$I in good prosperite and helth, thankyd be god of hys mercy, who
$I haue you in hys kepyng. Yeuen vnde our signet, in our sayd
$I town of Caen, the vte day of Septembre.
$I   Ferthermore, aftir the date of this lettres, our Castell of Caen ys
$I yolden to vs, and hostages leyd upon this condicion, but yif they
$I be rescowed bi bataille to be youen vn-to us by oure aduersaire
$I of france, other his son called the Dolphin, other therle of
$I Ermenak, be the xix day of the sayd moneth of Septembre.
