<The Queen of Bohemia to Sir E. Nicholas (1654),
<Memoirs of John Evelyn, ed. Bray, vol. V, pp. 198-199.

$I The Queen of Bohemia to Mr. Secretary Nicholas.
$I Hagh, Sep: 15. (1654.)
$I Mr. Secretarie, I assure you your letters are always verie
$I welcome to me. I hope before this come to you, you will
$I receaue a pacquet from Scotland which came to Roterdam,
$I and ould Will. Kepley caries himself to Aix. I shall be

<p. 199

$I verie glad to know what news it brings, because heere is
$I againe news of Monkes being beaten, which a man of
$I Midletons writes to Straghen from Stranaven or such a
$I name, and from my Lo. of [illegible], and that all long for
$I the King. Stone is at last here, he saith that Cromwell
$I will be now either King or Emperour, I wish him the
$I latter, he has heard nothing of Bamfeild, but I easilie
$I beleeue he is honnest enough to be well used by Cromwell,
$I he tells the Fleet as you hear, but it will not be beleeued
$I heere. This day the assemblie of Hollande begins. theire
$I agent in Sueden writt to the States Generall, that S=r= George
$I Fleetwood, brother to him that is Leftenant of Irland,
$I tolde him that he knew Cromwell had saide he woulde
$I keepe the peace with the States no longer then he found it
$I good for his interests, and woulde break with the first oc_casion
$I that he can for the good of his deseins. These of
$I Hollande are verie angrie at the agent for writing this:
$I those that have seene the letter tolde it me. it is so late as
$I I can say no more, but ame euer
$I Your most affectionat frend.
$I I pray remember me to my lord Wentworth, I have not
$I time to answer his letter but will doe it by the first post.
$I 15=o= 7=bris= 1654. R: 7/17    Qu: of Bohemia.
