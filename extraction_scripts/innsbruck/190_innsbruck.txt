<Ann Poole to her sister Isabel Plumpton (1551-52),
<Plumpton Correspondence, ed. Thomas Stapleton, 1839,
<LETTER XXXII, p. 258.

$I To her worshipful sister Mistres Plompton, at Plompton Hall, deliver these.
$I Right worshipful sister, after most harty and loving commenda_tions,
$I with like desire of your good health and the long continu_ance
$I of the same; this shall bee to give you most harty thanks for
$I al your gentlnes unto mee shewid, and in especially for your good_nes
$I shewid unto John Pool my son: he hath you most hartily
$I commended, as your poor kinsman and bedesman, and he desires
$I you, and I both, to take no displeasur with him for his long tar_riing
$I here, for as yet he is at no point for his childs part of my
$I husbands goods. Therfore, I desire you, as my trust is, that you will
$I bee so good Aunt unto him. Also I desir to have me commended
$I to my sister Clare and to my son Dennis, sending him Gods bless_ing
$I and mine, and to al other my kin and frends. Also my son
$I Richard and John commends them unto you and their aunt Clare,
$I and to Master Dennis, and to al other their good frends. And so
$I Jhesu preserve you. From Rodburn the 6 of March.
$I By your sister to her power
$I Ann Poole.

<(6 March 1551-2.)
