<Anne Lady Bacon to Mister Parr,
<(1613),
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<number 5, p. 9.

$I Mr PARR,—I have received your letter, and I
$I am very sory that any losse of favour or otherwise
$I any hurt should be to you, I am sure my letter
$I could import no such thing. I cannot give you so
$I large an answer to every poynt in your letter as
$I wear fytt, but you shall hear from me againe very
$I shortly when I have spoaken to my sonne, when
$I I will truly answer your letter from poynt to
$I poynt. I praye you cōmend my love to the Lady
$I Cornwaleys, whose love I desyre to continew,
$I though she will not yeelde it me in that sort I
$I desyer. And thus, in hast, fare well. God hath
$I the disposing of all things, and to Him .I cōmitt
$I this and all other. Yor assured
$I ANNE BACON.

<[1613]

$I To my assured freind Mr Parr.
