<Cromwell to an unnamed lord (1540),
<Life and Letters of Thomas Cromwell, ed. Merriman, no. 337, pp. 249-50.

$I   Aftre my right hartie comendacions vnto your lordship
$I wheras I am enformed that sir Richard Smithe parson of
$I Langom in the contie of Pembroke is accused and laid in
$I prison by the bailif ther for certain wordes spokyn sounding
$I to be treson against the Kinges maiestie These shalbe to
$I require you with convenient celeritie [to] send as well for the
$I said parson as other his accusors and vpon examinacion hade
$I and due proves and the said Smithe found gyltye therin he to
$I remain in prison to suche tyme as the kinges further plesor
$I shalbe known therin, and in the case his said accusors can make

<p. 250

$I no dew prove of the same that then he finding sufficiente
$I sureties per bondes for his apperance at all tyms when he
$I shalbe called for by the kinges highnes or any of his honour_able
$I consill you suffre him to go at libertie
