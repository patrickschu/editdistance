<Agnes Plumpton to Sir Robert Plumpton (1504a),
<Plumpton Correspondence, ed. Stapleton, pp. 186-87.

$I To the worshipfull Robart Plompton, knight, be these delivered in
$I                              hast.
$I   Right worshipfull Sir, in my most hartiest wyse I recommend me
$I unto you, desiring to here of your prosperytie and welfare, and
$I good spede in your matters, the which I marvell greatly that
$I I have no word from you. Sir, I marvell greatly that ye let
$I the matter rest so long, and labors no better for your selfe, and
$I ye wold labor it deligently. But it is sayd that ye be lesse for_ward,
$I and they underworketh falsly; and it is sene and known
$I by them, for they thinke to drive that they may take the Whit_sonday
$I ferme: and so it is sayd all the country about. Sir, I be_sech
$I you to remember your great cost and charges, and myne, and
$I labor the matter that it myght have an end, for they have taken
$I on capias and delivered for certayne of your tenants. And so they
$I have taken Edmund Ward at Knarsbrough and arrest him; the
$I which is a great nossen in the country, that they shall get such
$I prosses, and ye dow none to them, but lett them have there
$I mynd fullfilled in every case. And the other tenaunts cannot
$I pays ther housses, but the shalbe cagid; and also willing none
$I of your servants shall not pas the dowers, but they mon be tro_bled.
$I And also they have stopped the country, that ther will no
$I man deale with any of your servants, nether to bye wod, no nor
$I nothing els. Therfore, I pray you that ye will get some comand_ment 
$I to the Scherefe that the prosses may be stoped. Also, Sir,
$I I send you the copy of the letter that came from the Undershe_refe,
$I and the copy of the causes, and the letter that come from
$I William Ellison; the which I had mynd in for loyssing of Ed_mund 
$I Ward, for I have gotten him forth by the wayes of William
$I Ellyson. And also, Sir, I am in good health, and all the children
$I (blessed be Jesu) and all your children prayes you for your daly
$I blessing. And all your servants is in good health, and prays dely_gently

<p. 187

$I for your good speed in your matters. And also it is sayd
$I that they have cagments for them that hath bought the wood, that
$I they dare not deale therwith. For without ye get some comaunde_ment,
$I I wott not how your house shalbe kept, for I know not
$I wherof to levy one penyworth. No more at this tyme, but the
$I Trenietie keepe you. From Plompton in hast, the xii day of
$I Aprill.
$I                                    By your wyfe,
$I    (12 April 1504.)                    Dame AGNES PLOMPTON.
