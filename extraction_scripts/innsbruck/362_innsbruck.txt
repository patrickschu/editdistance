<Anne Lady Meautys to Jane Lady Bacon.
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<(1636)
<number 190, pp. 286-287.

$I DEARE SISTER,
$I ALTHOUGH I haue wriet diuers letters vnto y°,
$I of wch as yet I haue not receiued any answare of
$I them, yet such is my affection to y°, yt I can omit
$I noe vpportunitie to doe y° seruies. Mr Meautys is
$I in ye feeld before ye Conac, yt ye enime hath taken
$I this summer agin from vs. I haue not seene him
$I this halfe yeare, and I feere a winter campayn will
$I detaine him ye longer from mee, in yt hee hath ye
$I command ouer those companies yt lies there. Ye
$I Queene of Bohemia is now in ye Hage; she was
$I pleased to doe me yt honor to be godmother to my
$I child, ye wch I was ignorrant of vntill ye Queene's
$I retorne from Rine. I told her Mtie yt y° was ye
$I other godmother, ye wch shee was very well pleased
$I to vnderstand; her Matie telling of mee yt shee did
$I know y° to bee a lady yt was indued wth a great
$I dell of honour and virtue, & yt in her knowledg
$I y° did loue yor brother very well, wch was a thing
$I most commandible in y°: indeede, Sister, her Matie
$I doth use you wth much fauor, and hath promised
$I me yt what soeuer doth lie in her power to doe
$I mee good shee will not faile to perform it: shee
$I was pleased to giue vnto my littell Jane a fine
$I present, wch Mr Meautys did send mee from Arn_heim.

<p. 287

$I Deare Sister, in my other letters I did
$I desier y° to send vs ouer ye monie; wch, if y° please,
$I should bee very wellcome vnto vs. Thus, wishing
$I much happiness vnto y° and yors,
$I I remaine redy to doe y° seruise in all
$I True affection to my end,
$I       ANNA MEAUTYS.
$I Deft, ye 9th of June 1636.
$I Deare Sister, if y° please, send mee word how
$I my Hercules doth, to whome I send my blessing.
$I To the Lady Bacon, at Culford.
