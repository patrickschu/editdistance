<Robert Daborne to Henslowe: Sale of Daborne's estate (1613),
<The Alleyn Papers, ed. Collier, pp. 63-64.

$I    M Hinchlow, I wrote a leter to M=r= Griffyn requestinge
$I thearin y=r= awnswer, and end to those businesses and debts be_twixt
$I us, but I cannot hear from him. My desyre was y=t=
$I eather y=u= would be my paymaster for another play, or take xl.
$I of y=t= mony we hav had into y=r= hands agayne, and security for
$I the rest. S=r=, it is not unknown to y=u= y=t= I could and had good
$I certeynty of means before I wrote unto y=u=, which upon hopes
$I of y=r= love I forsooke, and must now, if y=u= and I had ended,
$I return to them agayne; for my occations, untill I have made
$I sale of y=t= estate I have, ar soe urgent, y=t= I can forbear no longer,
$I whearfor I pray, S=r=, of y=r= much ffryndship, doe me one curtesy
$I more till Thursday, when we deliver in o=r= play to y=u=, as to lend
$I me twenty shillings, and upon my ffayth and Christianyty I
$I will then  or giv y=u= content, or secure y=u= to the utmost farthing
$I y=u= can desyre of me. S=r=, I pray of all y=r= gentlenes deny not
$I this curtesy to me; and if y=u= fynd me not most just and hon_nest

<p. 64

$I to y=u=, may I want a ffrynd in my extremyty. It is but
$I till thursday I request y=u= hearin, and so rest
$I                                            at y=r= commaund,
$I                                                     ROB. DABORNE.
$I S=r=, y=u= hav a receipt of myne for twenty
$I shilllings, which I sent y=u= by the water_man
$I at the cardinalls hatt: that or this
$I shall sufficiently giv y=u= assurance.
$I                         witnes MOYSES BOWLER.
$I  30 July, 1613.
