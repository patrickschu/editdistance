$I 22 July 1583.
$I Mr. Broughton, I have sent this bearer unto you upon very
$I ernest occasion to suply my want. I have not yet, according to my L.
$I of Huntingdon's promise, receaved my allowance for the last quarter;
$I wherupon I am driven to be behind hand. I therefore desire you
$I to help me at this present wth xlli. to supply me necessarie wants
$I here, els I shalbe dryven to go up and seke my allowance my self.
$I I am further to desire you to further the cause of my Chaplin, Bate,
$I on whom I have bestowed the parsonage of Baxterley, that he may
$I have my presentacon confirmed by the feoffees. Thus wth my
$I harty comendacions to yor self and all at Bliethfeld, I bid you
$I farewell;   this xxijth of July 1583.
$I Yors
$I (Signed) R. ESSEX.
