<The Countess of Denbigh to Jane Lady Bacon.
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<(1630-31)
<number 153, pp. 237-238.

$I MADDAM,
$I THOUGH I have not the honor to be acquainted
$I with you, yet I must give you thankes for this
$I favour that you bestow uppon my deare cosen in
$I intertaining her for your daughter, in whome, be_fore
$I it be longe, I know you will thinke yourselfe
$I happy; and though her family be unfortunate, yet

<p. 238

$I you will find as true an affection as your hart can
$I desire: and if there be any thoughts remaine in
$I your mind of the disobedience of your sonne, I
$I beseech you to blotte it out, and be confident that
$I I shall esteeme him as one of my owne, and what
$I is in my power shall not be wanting to do him
$I service; and to yourself there lives none that shall
$I be more affectionatly yours,
$I Your faithful and humble servant,
$I             S. DENBIGH,
$I  [1630-31.]
$I For my deare and worthy freinde the La. Bacon, these.
