<The Countess of Bedford to Jane Lady Cornwallis
<(1616),
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<number 27, p. 37.

$I THIS day I could not meet you because the rest
$I of our Company could not be ther, and tomorrow
$I my La. of Roxborough's busnes, whom I must
$I not forsake, will passe a triall, so as I can neither
$I goe my jorney nor hope to see you, if you goe
$I away on Wensday, before your retorne into the
$I contry, which I am extream sory for; but I will
$I in the morninge send to you againe eyther my
$I farewell or to reseave the good newse that you
$I goe not so soone, which if you doe not, I will, God
$I willing, com to you on Wensday, till when I kisse
$I your hands. In extream hast, with the best affec_tion
$I of Your most faithfull freind,
$I L. BEDFORD

$I Grenwidge, this Monday night att 7 o'clock [1616].
