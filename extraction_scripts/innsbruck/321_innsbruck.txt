<The Countess of Bedford to Mister Bacon.
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<(1624)
<number 75, pp. 111-112.

$I Sr.
$I I SEE your freinds had need be moderat in ther
$I requests to you, that have so liberal a hand in
$I the satisfieinge them; for my part, I must give you
$I the discouragement to say you have gotten nothing by
$I your care so plentifully to furnishe me with what I

<p. 112

$I desired, but a bare acknowledgement of my thank_fullness;
$I since my desier to deserve well of you,
$I and sence of the obligations I ought you, wear att
$I that height before, as they can reseave no increase :
$I yett this do we the right to believe, that though
$I this must stand at a stay, my endevors shall not
$I do so, if ever they may find such employment as
$I may witnesse me to be in deed as well as in words,
$I Sir, the thankefullest and most assured of your
$I freinds,       L. BEDFORD.
$I Bedford House, in haste, this 6th of November [1624].
$I When you come next to town we shall be able
$I to shew you some good new pieses of painting at
$I Harington House.
$I To my much esteemed freind Mr Bacon, att Broome.
