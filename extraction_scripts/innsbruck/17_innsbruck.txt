<Alys, Lady Sudeley to Thomas Stonor (1431),
<Stonor letters and papers 1290, ed. Kingsford, no. 53, p. 47.

$I    Right trusty and entierly welbeloved frend y commaund me unto
$I you: and, where as y of singler trust in you have before this
$I enfeffed you with other in my Maners, londes and tenements
$I withyn dyvers shires, wole and hertely prey you, for gret
$I consideracions and causes touching my worship and gret profyt,
$I that ye seale the deedes, made yn youre name and other, of the
$I seid Maners to suche persons as be named in the same, wheche
$I seid deedes the berer of this shall shewe unto you, as my full
$I trust ys and hathe be unto you, like as the berer hereof shall
$I enfourme you: to whom y prey you geve credence. And, sir, yf
$I ther be anything that y may do for you in any mater in tyme
$I comyng, y wole do yt with all myn hert, and that knoweth God,
$I who have you in his blessed kepyng. Wreten at Sudely the iiij
$I day of Avrell.
$I    Alys, lady Sudeley.
$I    To the worshipfull and my trusty frend Thomas Stoner.
