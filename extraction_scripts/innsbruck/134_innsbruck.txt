<Margaret Queen of Scotland to Cardinal Wolsey (1515),
<Original Letters, Illustrative of English History, vol. 2, ed. Ellis,
<LETTER XLVI, p. 128.

$I My lorde Cardnal I comand me hartly to zou, and
$I I am glad to here that ze doo vell, and I have ressauet
$I a letter from you and a nother from my lorde Dakers
$I vher of I beleffe you know the effeyt. Ze vryte to me
$I that sych parssons as he sent for my maters in to
$I Scotland trust to be vell ansuerd, and hath sent to me
$I sysch vrytengs as thay sent to hym. But my lorde I
$I am gladar of the tydengs that the Kyngs grace teld
$I me of the Duk of Albane, that he vyl com hyther,
$I vysche I besech God may be trw, but I dred it be
$I not. My lorde I thynke ryght longe vyhil I speke
$I vyth you, for next the Kyngs grace my most trust is
$I in you, and you may doo me moste good of any, and I
$I pray you my lorde as soon as any body comes out of
$I Scotland that you vyl send me vorde, for I thynke
$I long tyl I here tydengs. No more: but God have
$I zou in hys kypeng
$I Zour frend
$I MARGARET R.
