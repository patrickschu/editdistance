<Elizabeth Lady Cornwallis to Jane Lady Bacon.
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<(1630-31)
<number 154, pp. 238-239.

$I DEERE MADAME,
$I As I concluded with your Lap at my coming
$I from you, so will I continue to pray to God to
$I give you helth and us your favor, in which we
$I shall bee at the highte of our ambytion, to the
$I obtaning wherof we shall never willingly omightt
$I any thing that may witnes our desiers therto; and
$I to that end I will endevor all I can the speedy
$I getting of the 3 thousand pounds which the King
$I and Queen hath ben plesed to promis us, the
$I which so soon as we have we shall most gladly
$I present to your Lap; till when I besech you ex_sept
$I of our desiers, and by thes my humble sarvis

<p. 239

$I and thanks for the noble welcom and favor which,
$I for my gratious mistrise's sake, at this time your
$I Lap gave me. But, Madam, I hope the next time
$I will bee for your sonne and my owne; in confidens
$I wherof I will ever bee,
$I Yor Lap's most affectionat daughter to
$I co(ande,    E. CORNWALLEIS.
$I [1630-31.]
$I Madame, Sir Thomas Stafford, my cosen Vil_lers,
$I and Mr Elleiot all kis your Lap's hands, and
$I humbly thank your Lap for your coach.
$I For the Lady Bacon, my much honored mother, these.
