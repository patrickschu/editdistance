<The Countess of Bedford to Jane Lady Cornwallis.
<(1618),
<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<number 41, pp. 58-59.

$I DEAR CORNEWALLIS,
$I I MUST not lett this bearer retorne without my
$I intreaty to you that to mine you will joyne your
$I thankes to Mr Bacon for the favor he hath donne
$I me in furnishing me with such helpes for my
$I garden, and lett me beg itt of you both that you
$I will believe that I shall be gladder to deserve then
$I reseave obligacions from you, though the demon_strations
$I of good will to me are not wellcommer

<p. 59

$I from any then your selves, whos love I infinitely
$I prise, and requite with the best affection of
$I     Your most affectionat and faithfull freind,
$I L. BEDFORD.
$I Bedford House, in haste, this 6th of November [1618].
$I To my noble and worthy freind the Lady Cornewallis, att Broome.
