<William, Prior of Durham to a friend (1451),
<The Priory of Coldingham, ed. Raine, pp. 170-71.

$I    Right welbiloved frennde, I grete yow hertly wele, thankynge
$I you entierly for all your diligent labours and favours
$I doon and shewide to me and my brether afortyme; praying
$I you of your goode and effectuall contynuance. For as mykil
$I as I have written befor tyme to your cousin maistre John Laxe,
$I our procuratour in the courte, for our celle of Coldingham, in the
$I whilke mater I had no certayn knawledge what labour or
$I soott is maade agayns us, therfor I pray yow that yhe will write

<p. 171

$I tendirly to the said M. John Laxe, that he walde certifye me by
$I writynge how the matier is prosecut in the courte; and in the
$I mean tyme I sall provide for necessary costage and ex_pense
$I for the goode spede and fortherannce of the same, with
$I the grace of our Lorde, who have you, etc. Writen, etc. iiij
$I day of May.
