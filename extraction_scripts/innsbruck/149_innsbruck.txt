<Lady Rocheford to Secretary Cromwell (1824),
<Original Letters, ed. Ellis, Vol. II,
<LETTER CXXIV, pp. 67-68.

$I Mayster Secretory, as a power desolat wydow
$I wythout comffort, as to my specyall trust under God
$I and my Pryns, I have me most humbly recommendyd
$I unto youe; prayng youe, after your accustemyd gentyll
$I maner to all them that be in suche lamentabull case
$I as I ame in, to be meane to the Kyngs gracyous Hygh_nes
$I for me for suche power stuffe and plate as my hus_bonde
$I had, whome God pardon; that of hys gracyous
$I and mere lyberalyte I may have hyt to helpe me to
$I my power lyviyng, whiche to his Hyghnes ys nothynge
$I to be regardyd, and to me schuld be a most hygh
$I helpe and souccor. And farther more, where that the

<p. 68

$I Kyngs Hyghnes and my Lord my father payed great
$I soms of money for my Joynter to the Errell of Wylt_chere
$I to the some off too thowsand Marks, and I not
$I assuryd of no more duryng the sayd Errells naturall
$I lyff then one hundreth Marke; whyche ys veary hard
$I for me to schyffte the worldd wythall. That youe wyll
$I so specyally tender me in thys behalff as to enforme
$I the Kyngs Hyghnes of these premysses, wherby I may
$I the more tenderly be regardyd of hys gracyous per_sone,
$I youre Worde in thys schall be to me a sure
$I helpe: and God schall be to youe therfore a sure re_ward,
$I whyche dothe promes good to them that dothe
$I helpe powere forsaken Wydos. And bothe my prayer
$I and servys schall helpe to thys duryng my naturall
$I lyff, as most bounden so to doo, God my wyttnes;
$I whoo ever more preserve you.
$I JANE ROCHEFORD.
