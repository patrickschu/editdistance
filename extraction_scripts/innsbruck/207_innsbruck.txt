<(1580)

$I v July 1580.
$I Mr. Broughton, I have bene some what troubled wth this
$I newe disease wch makes men both in London and Cambridge contyn_nuallie
$I to barke; but I am God be thanked hartie and mery almost
$I released from the same. This bearer old Lake wth his felowe have
$I bene wth me at Cambridge wch have made suite unto me for newe
$I livaryes, their old being quite worne; Wherefore I desire you to
$I take order for them that they may speadilie be provided for.
$I    Thus wth my hartie commendacons to Mr. Bagot & his wif yor
$I self and yor partner I bid you farewell. Cambridge this vth of July 1580.
$I    The night I returned from you my throte was sore, wch sorenes
$I was the messinger of the cold.
$I    Mr. Broughton I pray you send me shortly some money because
$I I thinke it wilbe longe ever I here from you.
$I    This commencement hath dronke me dry.
$I    I comend me to you all at Blithfield.
$I (Signed) R. ESSEX.
