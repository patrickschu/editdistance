<The Private Correspondence of Jane Lady Cornwallis,
<(1630-31)
<number 148, pp. 231-232.

$I MADAM,
$I BOTH dutie and desire dooth, by the asshurans
$I that your soon hath euer gifen me of your Lap's
$I goodnes and loue to him, giue me confidens by
$I thes to beg your blessinge and his pardon, hoo

<p. 232

$I thinks himselfe most vnhappy in your Lap's dis_plesure,
$I which I most humbly beseech yor Lap to
$I forgeef to him, that so much loufs and honõrs you
$I as that, withthout your Lap's loue and fauor restorde
$I to him, hee will neuer ioye in any thing ether
$I hee dooth ore shall posses; therfor, I besech your
$I Lap, let vs not any loungger suffer the want of
$I that which will make vs so infinitly happy: and
$I thoue, by want of the good fortune of being known
$I to your Lap, I coulde not hitherto hope of any fauor
$I from you, yet now my indeuors shall euer bee
$I such towards you as that I will all wais aprooue
$I my selfe to bee
$I Yor Lap's most obedient daughter and humble
$I saruant,    ELIZA CORNWALLEIS.
$I [1630-31.]
$I Madam, if your Lap will be plesed to make vs
$I so happie as to let vs know when we shall haue
$I the honõr of seing you heare, we will com down to
$I fetch you vp, or wait of yor Lap whersoeuer you
$I will apoynt; and so ons more I kis your hands, and
$I by all humble intreattis beg your Lap's pardon.
$I For my much honored mother the Lady Bacon, thes.
