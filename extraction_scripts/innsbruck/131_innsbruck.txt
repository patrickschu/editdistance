<Mary Queen of France to Wolsey (1514),
<after the death of her husband Louis the Twelfth,
<Original Letters, Illustrative of English History, ed. Henry Ellis,
<vol. 2, letter XLI, pp. 120-121.

$I My nanne good Lord, I recomend me to yow and
$I thankyng yow for yo=r= kynde and lovyng Letter, dys_syryng
$I yow of yo=r= good contenevans and good les_sones
$I that yow hathe gyffen to me; my lord, I pray
$I yow as my trust ys in yow, for to remember me to the
$I Kyng my brother, for sowche causses and bessynes
$I as I have for to do; for as now I have no nother
$I to put my trust in but the Kyng my brother and yow.

<p. 121

$I And as yt shall ples the Kyng my brother and hys
$I Counsell I wolbe horderd. And so I pray yow, my
$I lord, to show hys Grace, seyng that the Kyng my
$I howsbande ys departed to God, of whos sole God
$I pardon. And wher as yow a vyse me that I shulde
$I macke no promas, My Lord, I trust the Kyng my
$I brother and yow wole nat reken in me sovche chyld_hode.
$I I trust I have so horderd my selffe so sens
$I that I came hether, that I trust yt hathe ben to the
$I honar of the Kyng my brother and me, sens I come
$I hether, and so I trust to contenew. Yff ther be any
$I thynge that I may do for yow I wold be glade for to
$I do yt in thys partes. I shalbe glade to do yt for yow.
$I No more to you at thys tyme but Jhs preserve yow.
$I Wretten at Pares the x. day of January 1515.
$I By yowr lowyng
$I frende MARY
$I QUENE OF FRANCE.
$I To My Lorde of Yorke.
