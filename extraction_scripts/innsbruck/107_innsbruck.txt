<Agnes Plumpton to Sir Robert Plumpton (1502),
<Plumpton Correspondence, ed. Stapleton., p. 171.

$I To the worshipfull Sir Robart Plompton, knight, be thes byll delivered
$I                             in hast.
$I   Right worshipful Sir, in my most harte wyse I recommend me
$I unto you, desiring to here of your welfare and good speed in
$I your matters. I and all your children is in good health (blessed
$I be Jesu). And, Sir so it is, as God knowes, that I have mayd as
$I great labor as was possible for me to make, to content your mynd
$I in all causes; and now I have mayd the usance of xx=li=, and sent
$I you with Thomas Bekerdike to content where ye know. And
$I I pray you to send some wrytting to Thomas Meryng for the
$I repayment of the money and your discharg. Sir, it is so that
$I my lord Archbishop hath indytt my sone William and xvi of his
$I servants, on tewsday was a senit. But Anthony Cliforth gave
$I in the bill of dytement against my sone and his servants, but the
$I quest would not endyte them. But my lord Archbishop caused
$I them; or els he bad them tell who wold not, and he should
$I ponishe them, that all oder should take insample. And I cannot
$I get the copie of the indytement, for my lord hath it in his
$I hands. No more at this tyme. The Lord preserve you. From
$I Plompton in hast, this St. Thomas day.
$I                                    By your wife, dame
$I     (21 Dec. 1502.)                AGNES PLOMPTON.
