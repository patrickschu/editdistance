<Agnes Plumpton to Thomas Everingam (1502),
<Plumpton Correspondence, ed. Stapleton, p. 172.

$I To master Thomas Everingam, be this delivered in hast.
$I   Cousin Thomas Everyngham, I recomennd me unto you, thanking
$I you of your good mynd and will at all tymes; praying you, that ye
$I will take the labor and payne upon you to come and speake with
$I me betwyxt this and tewsday next, as my speciall trust is in you;
$I and that ye faylle not therof, as I may dow for you as much in
$I tyme to come. No more at this tyme, but the Trenyte kepe you.
$I From Plompton in hast, this Sant Maury day.
$I                                         By yours at my power
$I     (15 Jan. 1502-3.)                  /   Dame AGNES PLOMPTON.
