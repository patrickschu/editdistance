<Margaret Queen of Scotland to King Henry VIII (1513),
<Original letters illustrative of English history, ed. Henry Ellis, vol. 2,
<Letter XXV, pp. 64-65.

$I Richt excellennt, richt hie and mithty Prince, our
$I derrest and best belovit Brothir, We commend ws
$I unto zow in oure maist hertlie wise. Zoure ambassa_doure
$I Doctoure West deliverit ws zoure lovyng let_trez
$I in quhiwis ze schew ws that quhare ze harde of
$I our seiknes ze tuke grete hevynes. Deerest brother
$I We ar greitly rejosit that we se ze have respect to oure

<p. 65

$I disese, and thairfor We geve zou oure hartlie thankis,
$I and zoure writing is to ws gude confort. We can
$I nocht beleve that of zoure mynd or be zoure command
$I we are sa friendly delt with in oure faderis Legacy,
$I quharof we wald nocht have spokyn nor writing had
$I nocht the Doctoure now spokyn to ws of the sammyn
$I in his credence. Oure husband knawis it is withhaldin
$I for his saik, and will recompens ws safer as the
$I Doc_toure schew him. We ar eschamit thairwith; and
$I wald God nevir word had bene tharof. It is nocht
$I worth sic estimacioun as is in zoure divers lettrez of
$I the sammyn; and we lak na thing; our husband is
$I evir the langer the better to ws, as knawis God, quha
$I richt hie and michty Prince, oure derrest and bestbe_lovit
$I brothir, have zou in governaunce. Geven under
$I oure Signete at oure Palace of Linlithg=w=. the xj day
$I of Aprile,
$I Your lowynn syster
$I MARGARET

$I To the richt excellennt richt
$I hie and michty Prince oure
$I derrest and best belovit bro_thir
$I the King of England.
