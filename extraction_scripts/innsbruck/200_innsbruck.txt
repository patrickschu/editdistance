<Sir Philip Sidney to the Earl of Leicester (1573),
<Sir Philip Sidney, ed. Feuillerat, no. 3, pp. 77-78.

$I   Ryghte honorable and my singular good Lorde and Unkle,
$I this bearer havinge showed me the woorkes he dothe cary into
$I Englande gave me ocasion humble to sende these few woordes
$I unto yowr Lordeshippe, thoughe my wrytynge at this presente
$I unto yow by an Englisshe gentleman that dothe now returne,
$I take away any other cause of Englarginge the same. This bearer
$I hathe promised me to lett no man see that whiche he cariethe
$I untill he have showed them unto yowr Lordeshipp. If they
$I may seeme unto yowr Lordeshippe unworthie of which I shoolde
$I wryte unto yow, I do most humblie beseche yow to condemne
$I therein nothinge but my ignorance, whiche bendinge it selfe
$I wholie to content yow, if it do erre, I hope yowr goodness will

<p. 78

$I suffer the dutifull mynde, to recompence the wante of judge_ment,
$I whiche beinge all that I have at this tyme to trooble yow
$I witheall, I will most lowlie committ yow to the Eternalls pro_tection.
$I   Frome Francforde this 18=th= of Marche A=o= 1572[-3]
$I              Yowr moste humble and moste
$I                             obediente nephew
$I                                    Philip Sidney.
$I   To the ryghte honorable and my singular unkle, the Earle
$I of Lecestre.
