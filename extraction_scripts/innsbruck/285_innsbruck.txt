$I DEARE CORNEWALLIS,
$I THE K. of Denmarcke's unexpected coming
$I hath constrayned me to defer my setting forward
$I towards Rutland from the 8lh of August to the
$I tenth, which is Wensday comse'night, and so itt
$I will be the llth before I shall be at Huntingdon,
$I whear I promis myselfe the contentment of meeting
$I you; whearof to have a confirmation, and that you
$I may not be ignorant of this alteration, I send
$I this bearer, whom lett me intreatt you to dispatch
$I as soone as you may: and suffer not any thing lesse
$I then necessity to change your purpos, and Mr
$I Bacon's, to take this jorney for her sake that
$I will ever be to you both a most assured and
$I affectionate fremd, L. BEDFORD,
$I My mistres comanded me not to forget her
$I kindest comendations to you.
$I Bedford House, in haste, this Saterday morneinge, 30th July [1614].
$I To my worthy freind the Lady Cornewallis.
